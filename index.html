üîπ 3. Post Availability Screen

When to use:
To let the system know when your truck and driver are available for new routes.
Steps (with explanation):
  • Select Date of Availability
    What is this? The date when your truck and driver are free to take a job.
    How it works? The system will only match you with routes starting on or after this date.
  • Select Truck from dropdown
    What is this? Choose which truck you want to make available.
    How it works? The system checks the truck‚Äôs size/type (e.g., Box 22ft) and matches it with routes that require that truck type.
  • Select Driver from dropdown
    What is this? Assign a driver to the truck.
    How it works? The driver‚Äôs name will be linked to the availability, so 3PLs know who will operate the truck.
  • Enter Location ‚Üí City, State, ZIP code
    What is this? The starting point where the truck/driver will be available.
    How it works? Routes are matched based on this location. If a 3PL has a pickup near this area, your availability will show up.
  • Select Product Types
    What is this? The type of goods you can transport (e.g., Furniture, Electronics, General Cargo).
    How it works? Ensures you only get matched with routes carrying products you can handle.
  • Click Post
    What is this? The action to publish your availability.
    How it works? Once posted, your availability is visible to 3PLs in the system.
Result: Your availability is posted and visible to 3PLs, allowing them to assign you to matching routes.
Who should see this post and where?
Who? 3PL companies (logistics providers) who are looking for available trucks and drivers.
Where? In their Available Contractors/Trucks list inside the 3PL portal, filtered by location, date, truck type, and product type.

Steps (with explanation):
  • Company Name
    What is this? The legal or registered name of your company.

  • USDOT# (U.S. Department of Transportation Number)
    What is this? A unique number issued by the U.S. Department of Transportation.
    How it works? Required for any company that operates commercial vehicles across state lines. It tracks safety records, inspections, and compliance.
    Example: 1234567
  • USDOT Expiration Date
    What is this? The date when your USDOT registration expires.
    How it works? You must renew before this date to stay compliant. The system may warn you if it‚Äôs close to expiration.
  • Profile Description
    What is this? A short description of your company (e.g., ‚ÄúWe specialize in furniture delivery across Midwest states‚Äù).
    How it works? Helps 3PLs understand your services and strengths.
  • MCP Permits (Motor Carrier Permits)
    What is this? State-level permits required for operating commercial trucks in certain states (e.g., California MCP).
    How it works? You can add multiple permits with their number, expiration date, and state.
    Example: MCP #12345, Expiration: 2025-08-18, State: CA
Result: Your company profile is complete and compliant. 3PLs can see your verified details, and the system ensures you meet U.S. regulations for trucking and logistics.

üîπ 33. Stop Performance Report Screen

When to use:
To analyze stop-level performance ‚Äî how many stops were planned, completed, missed, or delayed, and why.
Top Summary Boxes (with explanation):
  • Total Planned Stops
    What is this? The total number of delivery stops planned.
    How it works? Adds up all stops from all routes.
    Why it matters? Shows the total workload.
  • Total Completed Stops
    What is this? The number of stops successfully delivered.
    How it works? Updates when a driver marks a stop as delivered.
    Why it matters? Shows how many deliveries were successful.
  • Total Incomplete Stops
    What is this? The number of stops that were not completed.
    How it works? Could be due to customer not available, wrong address, or driver error.
    Why it matters? Missed stops = unhappy customers + possible penalties.
  • Total Delayed Stops
    What is this? The number of stops delivered late.
    How it works? If a stop was scheduled for 10:00 AM but delivered at 12:00 PM, it counts as delayed.
    Why it matters? Delays affect customer satisfaction and 3PL trust.
Filters (with explanation):
  • Filter by Date Range
    What is this? Select a time period.
    How it works? Shows stop performance only for that period.
  • Filter by Warehouse
    What is this? Filter stops by warehouse ID or name.
    How it works? Useful if you work with multiple warehouses.
  • Filter by Status
    What is this? Filter by stop status (Completed, Incomplete, Delayed).
    How it works? Helps you focus on problem areas.
  • Filter by Final Rate Status
    What is this? Filter by payment status (Pending, Finalized).
    How it works? Useful for financial analysis of stops.
Stop Performance Details (with explanation):
  • Incomplete Reasons
    What is this? A list of reasons why stops were not completed.
    How it works? Examples: Customer not home, Wrong address, Refused delivery, Vehicle breakdown.
    Why it matters? Helps identify patterns in missed deliveries.
  • Delay Reasons
    What is this? A list of reasons why stops were delayed.
    How it works? Examples: Traffic jam, Weather, Late warehouse loading, Driver delay.
    Why it matters? Helps identify why deliveries are late.
Result: This screen shows how well you are performing at the stop level. If you have many incomplete stops, you need to improve reliability. If you have many delayed stops, you need to improve time management. A high completed stops rate = strong performance and more trust from 3PLs.
